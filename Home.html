Zero Hunger

Zero Hunger is one of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) aimed at ending hunger, achieving food security, improving nutrition, and promoting sustainable agriculture by 2030.

Why is Zero Hunger important?

Hunger and malnutrition are significant global issues, affecting millions of people worldwide. Achieving Zero Hunger not only ensures that everyone has access to sufficient and nutritious food but also contributes to poverty reduction, economic growth, and sustainable development.

How can we contribute to Zero Hunger?

  • Promote sustainable agriculture practices
  • Support small-scale farmers and rural communities
  • Reduce food waste and loss
  • Ensure equitable access to resources and opportunities
  • Advocate for policies that address food insecurity and malnutrition

Global Hunger Statistics

According to the Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO), approximately 690 million people worldwide are undernourished. This means one in every nine people do not have enough food to lead a healthy, active life.
